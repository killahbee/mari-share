Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is Mari-Share?

    2. Mari-Share is an online community matching people who grow marijuana in Washington, D.C., with other growers and users. Our goal is to bring people together from within the grassroots sharing economy that has emerged in D.C. due to the lack of a retail industry for marijuana.

  2. What's a gifter?

    3. If you grow marijuana in Washington, D.C. and are willing to share, you can become a leader in the marijuana sharing economy by becoming a gifter on Mari-Share.

  3. Why be a gifter?

    4. The fact that the government legalized without setting up a retail industry for marijuana has created something really cool in D.C. Maybe we should all grow in our own homes and share it with our neighbors like a tomato plant. As a gifter, you can propel the sharing economy movement forward. Become a gifter to:

    • Make new friends
    • Lead a movement
    • Pay it forward
    • Get feedback to hone your craft

  4. Is this legal?

    5. Yes. In Washington, D.C., it is legal for adults 21 years of age or older to:

    • Possess two ounces or less of marijuana;
    • Grow within their primary residence up to six marijuana plants, no more than three of which are mature;
    • Transfer one ounce or less of marijuana to another person as long as: 1) no money, goods, or services are exchanged; and 2) the recipient is 21 years of age or older; and
    • Consume marijuana on private property.

    Read more about Initiative 71

    Mari-Share is in strict accordance with the law and intended only for creating relationships among growers and users.

  5. What if I see illegal activity?

    6. If you see any illegal activity on a Mari-Share profile, in a message, or anywhere else on the site, please report it to report@mari-share.com immediately. Mari-Share reserves the right to remove any account without warning or explanation.

  6. Does it cost money?

    7. No. An account on Mari-Share is free. And you should never pay for marijuana using Mari-Share or at all in D.C.
